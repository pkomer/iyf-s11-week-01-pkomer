Intoduction

The zebra is one of Africa's most recognizable animals. Known for its distinctive black-and-white striped coat, the zebra belongs to the horse family and lives primarily in grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands.
These beautiful animals are an important part of Africa's wildlife and attract visitors from around the world.

Physical Characteristics

Zebras are medium-sized mammals with strong bodies, long legs, and upright manes. Their most unique feature is their striped coat. Each zebra has a different stripe pattern, making every individual unique.
Scientists often compare zebra stripes to human fingerprints because no two patterns are exactly the same. The scientific name for the plains zebra is Equus quagga. A lot of Zebras are protected by conservation organizations such as WWF.

Habitat and Distribution

Zebras are found in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, Botswana, and South Africa. Their habitats include grasslands, savannas, and some mountainous regions depending on the species.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Zebras are herbivores, meaning they eat plants. Their diet mainly consists of grass, but they may also eat leaves, shrubs, and bark when food is scarce.

Interesting Facts About Zebras
  • Every zebra has a unique stripe pattern.
  • Zebras can run at speeds of up to 65 kilometers per hour.
  • Zebras often travel long distances in search of food and water.
  • They have excellent eyesight and hearing.
  • A group of zebras is called a herd.
  • Zebras sleep standing up
